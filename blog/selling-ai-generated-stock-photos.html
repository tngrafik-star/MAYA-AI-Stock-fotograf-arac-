Guide to Selling AI-Generated Stock Photos: Midjourney, DALL-E, and SEO

📅 July 17, 2026 ⏱️ 9 min read ✍️ MayaListing Team

Artificial intelligence technologies have initiated a brand new era in the world of visual design and stock photography. High-quality images generated with tools like Midjourney, DALL-E 3, and Stable Diffusion are now reaching millions of buyers. But how can you sell these AI-generated images legally and profitably on stock photography websites? In this guide, we have compiled everything you need to know, from agency acceptance policies to quality standards and SEO optimization.

Which Stock Platforms Accept AI-Generated Images?

Not all stock photography sites share the same perspective on AI-generated content. Before uploading your portfolio, you must review the current licensing policies of the platforms:

1. Adobe Stock (Industry Leader)

Adobe Stock was the first and remains the most popular major platform to accept AI-generated images. However, it has very strict rules:

  • You must check the "Created using AI tools" tag for your upload.
  • You must select the content type as "Illustration" (not Photo).
  • Your titles or keywords must not make false claims of real-world authenticity.

2. Freepik

Freepik is one of the most active supporters of AI-generated content and even offers its own built-in AI generators. Thanks to its high download volume, it is an excellent channel to generate passive income from your AI art.

3. Shutterstock

Shutterstock has banned the upload of images generated by external AI tools like Midjourney or DALL-E. It only allows the licensing of images created directly with Shutterstock's own integrated AI generator to ensure copyright protections.

4. Getty Images / iStock

To avoid copyright and ownership risks, Getty Images and iStock enforce a strict ban on any externally sourced AI-generated content.

Preparing AI Images for Stock Agencies

You cannot upload raw AI outputs directly to stock websites. To pass the quality control process, follow these critical steps:

Upscale Your Images

Most AI generators produce images at lower resolutions, such as 1024x1024 pixels. Stock agencies require a minimum of 4 megapixels (typically around 2000x2000 pixels or higher). Use AI-powered upscaling software like Topaz Gigapixel AI, Upscayl, or similar tools to scale up your images by 4x without losing quality.

Quality Control & Retouching

Check your images for typical AI errors: six-fingered hands, distorted facial features, illogical object merges, and gibberish text. Retouch these areas using Photoshop or Canva to boost your acceptance rate.

SEO and Metadata Optimization for AI Art

Having a visually stunning image is not enough; buyers must be able to find it in search results. This is where title, description, and keyword optimization come in.

1. Crafting the Title

Your title should be descriptive and clearly define the content of the image. Mentioning that the image is AI-generated by adding "Generative AI" is considered an SEO best practice.

Example: "Generative AI illustration of a futuristic smart city at sunset, neon lights"

2. Selecting Keywords (Tags)

Add 20 to 30 keywords that describe the colors, objects, style, and concept of the image. For Adobe Stock, make sure your top 5 most important keywords are placed at the beginning of the tag list to boost search visibility.
